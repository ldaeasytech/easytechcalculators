Why Determine the Optimum Pipe Size?

Selecting the correct pipe diameter balances energy consumption and installation cost. Oversized pipes increase capital cost. Undersized pipes increase friction losses and pumping power. The optimum pipe diameter minimizes total annual cost.

Capital vs Operating Costs in Fluid Flow

Total annual cost consists of:

  • Capital Cost: Pipe material, fittings, installation
  • Operating Cost: Pumping power and energy consumption

Why Minimize Annual Cost?

The economic optimum occurs where:

Total Annual Cost = Annualized Capital Cost + Operating Cost

Using only operating cost = capital cost is incorrect because:

  • Energy prices vary over time
  • Equipment lifespan must be considered
  • Capital is invested upfront but recovered annually

The minimum annual cost method ensures economically optimal system design.

What is NPSH Available and NPSH Required?

Net Positive Suction Head (NPSH) is a critical parameter in pump system design. NPSH Available (NPSHa) is the total suction head available at the pump inlet, while NPSH Required (NPSHr) is the minimum suction head required by the pump to avoid cavitation.

For safe pump operation: NPSHa must always be greater than NPSHr.

Why is NPSH Important in Pumps?

Insufficient NPSH causes cavitation ‚Äî the formation and collapse of vapor bubbles inside the pump. This leads to vibration, noise, reduced efficiency, and severe impeller damage.

What is Cavitation?

Cavitation occurs when the local pressure at the pump suction drops below the vapor pressure of the liquid. Vapor bubbles form and violently collapse as pressure recovers inside the impeller.

  • Impeller pitting
  • Loss of flow rate
  • Reduced pump head
  • Mechanical seal damage
  • Shortened pump lifespan

General Rule to Prevent Cavitation

Always maintain a safety margin:

NPSHa ‚â• NPSHr + Safety Margin (typically 0.5 to 1 meter)

Increase NPSHa by:

  • Raising the liquid source level
  • Reducing suction pipe losses
  • Using larger suction pipe diameter
  • Lowering fluid temperature

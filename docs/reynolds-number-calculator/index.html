Understanding Reynolds Number

What is Reynolds Number?

Reynolds number is the ratio of inertial forces to viscous forces in a fluid. It predicts whether flow will be smooth (laminar) or chaotic (turbulent).

Why is 2300 Important?

For internal pipe flow: • Re < 2300 → Laminar • 2300–4000 → Transitional • > 4000 → Turbulent

Laminar vs Turbulent Flow

Laminar flow is orderly and layered. Turbulent flow contains eddies and mixing.

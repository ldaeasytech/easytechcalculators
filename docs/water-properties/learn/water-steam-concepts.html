Level 1 — Fundamentals & Intuition

  1. A cup of water is filled to the brim at 25 °C. If it is heated to 40 °C, what happens to the water level? Explain why.
  2. Why does ice float on liquid water, and what does this imply about density?
  3. At 1 atm and 150 °C, is water subcooled, saturated, or superheated? Explain your reasoning.
  4. Why is the specific gravity of liquid water close to 1 at room temperature?
  5. Which has higher density: liquid water or steam at atmospheric conditions? Why?

Level 2 — Trends & States

  1. How does the density of liquid water change with increasing temperature at constant pressure?
  2. Why does the viscosity of liquid water decrease as temperature increases?
  3. Why is only one independent property required to define a saturated state?
  4. During vaporization at constant pressure, what happens to entropy and why?
  5. Why are Cp and Cv nearly equal for liquid water but significantly different for gases?

Level 3 — Engineering Reasoning & Applications

  1. Why is superheated steam preferred over saturated steam at the inlet of a turbine?
  2. Why is liquid water an excellent heat transfer medium compared to steam?
  3. How do viscosity and density influence pumping power requirements?
  4. Why must engineers understand property trends rather than relying solely on tabulated or calculated values?
  5. How are temperature, pressure, and phase related for water according to the phase diagram?
